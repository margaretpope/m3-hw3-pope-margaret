About

John Clayton Mayer was born on October 16, 1977, in Bridgeport, Connecticut. He started paying guitar at an early age, eventually enrolling in Berklee College of Music. He dropped out after a couple of semesters to move to Atlanta to play music. While in Atlanta, he performed at the iconic Eddie's Attic where many other musicians have gotten their start. He recorded and released his first EP Inside Wants Out in 1999. In the early-2000s, Mayer signed with Columbia Records which released his first studio album, Room for Sqaures. This album procuded his hits, including "No SUch Thing." Mayer continued to gain popularity and went on to release seven more studio albums over the next two decades.

Although most of his career has been solo, he has been part of a few other bands. He played alongside Steve Jordan and Pino Palladino as the John Mayer Trio. The trio released one live albume together. Most recently, Mayer has played and toured with Bob Weir, Bill Kreutzmann, and Mickey Hart as Dead & Company, an interation of the Grateful Dead.
